About the Crop:

Potato (Solanum tuberosum) Kurfi Jyoti matures in 90 to 120 days from planting. It thrives in well-drained, loamy soil with a pH of 5.5 to 6.5. Each acre can yield approximately 20 to 30 tons of potatoes annually. The crop requires about 600 to 800 mm of water per growing season, with consistent moisture crucial for tuber development. Input costs, including seed, fertilizers, irrigation, and labor, typically range from ₹60,000 to ₹90,000 per acre. With market prices of ₹20 to ₹40 per kg, net profit can range from ₹2,00,000 to ₹8,00,000 per acre, depending on yield and market conditions. Effective management practices, such as proper irrigation and pest control, can maximize both yield and profitability.

  • Soil Composition: Well-Drained Loamy Soil with Ph 5.5 to 6.5
  • Water need per Acre: 600-800 mm per acre annually
  • Potential Dangers: Leptinotarsa decemlineata, Phytophthora infestans, Early Blight
  • Fertilizers: Ammonium Nitrate (33-0-0), Muriate of Potash, Zinc Sulfate
  • Pesticides: Copper Oxychloride, Streptomycin, Fipronil
  • Profit Per Acre: ₹2,90,000 to ₹9,25,000 per acre annually
